Upocoming Feautures

While Hubble focuses on visible and ultraviolet wavelengths, the upcoming James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) will observe the universe primarily in infrared light. Together, they will offer a complementary view of the cosmos, enabling more detailed studies of galaxies, stars, and planetary systems.

With regular assessments and potential updates to its instruments, Hubble is expected to continue its mission beyond its original lifespan. There are ongoing discussions about future servicing missions to keep Hubble operational well into the next decade.

Hubble will continue to play a crucial role in the exploration of exoplanets by studying their atmospheres and potential habitability. With planned upgrades, Hubble’s ability to analyze exoplanetary atmospheres in detail will improve, providing essential data for future interstellar missions.

Hubble will continue building its legacy as a premier space observatory. As new telescopes and missions emerge, Hubble’s extensive catalog of images and data will remain a priceless resource for future astronomers, serving as a foundation for ongoing and future discoveries.
