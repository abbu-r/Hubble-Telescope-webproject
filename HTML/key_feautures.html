Key Features

Hubble has captured some of the most distant objects ever observed, such as the Hubble Deep Field images, offering a glimpse into the early universe.

The data and images collected by Hubble are freely accessible to the public and scientific community, contributing to countless research projects worldwide.

Hubble has contributed to major discoveries in our understanding of the universe.

Hubble's advanced optical system captures high-resolution images of distant galaxies, nebulae, and star clusters.

Positioned 547 kilometers (340 miles) above Earth, Hubble avoids atmospheric distortion, providing clearer and more detailed images.

Hubble observes space in a range of wavelengths, including visible, ultraviolet, and near-infrared light.
